### Candidate 03 Pre-Audit Framing — Responsibility Cannot Be Outsourced

The browser response correctly distinguishes legitimate delegation from disappearance of accountability. Work, authority, responsibility, and bounded decisions may be allocated or delegated; automation and vendors may perform work; shared bodies may decide. The Value signal is that accountability must remain explicit before, during, and after those arrangements.

Two boundary cases must anchor the formal card:

- **Success without ownership:** an automated process can violate the candidate before any visible failure if continued success has allowed its owner, limits, monitoring duty, and escalation path to disappear.
- **Responsibility without authority:** Spark should not assign an individual accountability for systemic conditions they lack meaningful authority or ability to influence. Individual and organizational accountability must be distinguished and proportionate to role, authority, knowledge, and control.

**Audit caution:** `Responsibility Cannot Be Outsourced` is memorable but overbroad. It can imply that delegation is itself improper or that every person remains personally responsible for everything. The formal audit should test names such as `Accountability Must Have an Owner` or `Responsibility Must Remain Explicit` without adopting either before the full card and complete-set comparison.

**Required next action:** Build and test the seven-part Candidate 03 card. Do not assign retain, refine, merge, reject, or reopen until that audit is complete.

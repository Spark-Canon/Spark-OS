# Philosophy

The Philosophy area answers:

> What do we believe, and how should those beliefs shape Spark OS?

It contains the foundational beliefs that guide the Canon, Academy, governance, operations, AI behavior, and future software.

## Philosophy Framework

1. [`manifesto.md`](manifesto.md) — why Spark OS exists and what future it is trying to create
2. [`values.md`](values.md) — what Spark refuses to compromise
3. [`principles.md`](principles.md) — durable decision rules derived from accepted values
4. [`mental-models.md`](mental-models.md) — reusable ways of interpreting problems and trade-offs
5. [`glossary.md`](glossary.md) — canonical definitions for foundational language

## How the Documents Relate

The Philosophy layer should develop in sequence:

```text
Manifesto
   ↓
Values
   ↓
Principles
   ↓
Mental Models
   ↓
Shared Language
```

The Manifesto establishes purpose. Values protect that purpose. Principles translate those commitments into decision rules. Mental Models support judgment when applying them. The Glossary protects consistent meaning across every layer.

The relationship is directional, but not isolated. Later documents may reveal ambiguity in earlier ones and trigger deliberate review.

## Authority

Philosophy is more foundational than a course, procedure, software feature, AI prompt, or derivative output.

When an output conflicts with accepted Philosophy, the output should be reconsidered.

Philosophy does not replace verified mortgage knowledge, legal requirements, regulatory obligations, or accepted architectural decisions. It guides how Spark OS interprets, organizes, teaches, and applies them.

## Change Standard

Changes to accepted Philosophy should be deliberate because they may affect:

- Canon structure and interpretation
- Academy learning design
- governance and AI roles
- operating procedures
- software requirements and behavior
- terminology across the repository

A material change should identify downstream documents that may require review.

## Current Status

The Manifesto and Values are accepted and active foundations of Spark Philosophy.

Principles are the next Philosophy development phase. Mental Models and the Glossary remain structurally established but substantively in development.

Development remains sequential: Manifesto → Values → Principles → Mental Models → shared language. Each layer should constrain and clarify the next, while later documents may reveal ambiguity that warrants deliberate revision of an earlier document.

# Spark OS Manifesto

**Status:** Framework established — content in development  
**Authority:** Foundational philosophy

## Purpose

The Manifesto explains why Spark OS exists, what problem it is intended to solve, and what future it is trying to create.

It should give a reader enough context to understand the ambition behind Spark OS before they encounter its values, principles, Canon, Academy, governance, operations, or software.

## Relationship to Other Philosophy Documents

The Manifesto is the starting point for the Philosophy layer.

- The Manifesto explains **why Spark OS exists**.
- [`values.md`](values.md) defines **what Spark refuses to compromise**.
- [`principles.md`](principles.md) turns those values into **durable decision rules**.
- [`mental-models.md`](mental-models.md) defines **reusable ways of thinking**.
- [`glossary.md`](glossary.md) protects **shared meaning and terminology**.

The Manifesto should inspire and orient. It should not attempt to perform the work of the other documents.

## What Belongs Here

- the problem Spark OS is designed to solve
- the limitations of fragmented knowledge and disconnected systems
- the relationship between expertise, capability, judgment, teaching, operations, AI, and software
- the kind of professional and organization Spark OS is intended to develop
- the long-term promise of Spark OS
- statements that establish direction and meaning

## What Does Not Belong Here

- detailed mortgage guidance
- operating procedures
- software requirements
- AI role instructions
- course outlines or assessments
- short-term plans or sprint goals
- lists of values without explanation
- rules that belong in Principles

## Development Standard

The finished Manifesto should be:

- clear enough for a new team member
- ambitious without becoming vague
- durable across tools and platforms
- specific enough to guide future decisions
- concise enough to be read in one sitting
- aligned with the lived reality Spark intends to create

## Future Development

The next version of this document should answer:

1. What is broken about the way mortgage expertise is commonly captured, taught, and applied?
2. Why is memorization insufficient for professional capability?
3. Why must knowledge, learning, operations, AI, and software remain aligned?
4. What kind of broker and organization should Spark OS help create?
5. What does Spark OS promise to preserve and make possible?

A candidate central thesis for development is:

> Spark OS exists to turn scattered expertise into shared capability.
